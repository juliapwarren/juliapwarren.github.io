Comprehensive Assessment Project

Completed Fall 2019

Major project (revised). I have chosen an assignment I completed in my first semester in the TPC program to function as the centerpiece of my CAP. This is a literature review from a TPC research methods class. At that time, I knew very little about the field, so I chose to research a topic I knew a lot about: Google Docs! (Original submitted)

Framing and reflections. Looking back on that literature review nearly two years later. What do I think I was thinking?

Thank you for perusing my hard work. Below is the portfolio segment of my comprehensive assessment project. I've assembled a collection of then artifacts from my coursework to showcase and reflect on. 
  1. Brochure with style guide. Grade received: 20/20, brochure; 10/10, style guide. Looking back at this brochure, it is something I would have mocked up as a draft, but not submitted as final. However, I think it was the best team effort we could have put together online (this was a group project).
  2. Typesetting and Word styles project (with memo). Grade received: A (overall). The objective of this project was to typeset the content provided (a rich text source file and separate images and tables) in a length maximum, using a style guide influenced by ECU's branding standards. I really had fun playing around with concocting a style guide for this document, then applying it using Word's styles program, and finally, making it all *fit*. This is one of my favorite pieces of work I did in the program.
  3. Hypothetical technical communication department proposal. Grade received: 285/300. I remember feeling clueless as I drafted this, but this is the first project where I dove head first into Word's styling capabilities. I don't know that I'll ever find myself in the scenario of launching a department of a company, but this piece showcases my communication and information organization abilities.
  4. Brand strategy kit for community band. Grade received: 100/100. Also from my first semester in the program, this was actually my first effort at some graphic design. I am not particularly proud of the actual content I proposed (maybe it would look better on a live site), but again, I had fun applying branding guidelines and identifying problems through a competitive analysis.
  5. Media improvement proposal for local dance studio. Grade received: 150/150. This felt similar to the brand strategy kit above, with the exception of needing to scope out resources for long-term consideration. This demonstrates my ability to think outside the content and keep business priorities front and center.
  6. TPC ethics case study report on GenX. Grade received: 20/20. This is a good example of my ability to search literature and websites and find information that needs to be conveyed in more lay terms/contexts through a rhetorical framework.
  7. Business writing course programming report. Grade received: 94/100. This is a short review of some literature on teaching business writing. I am not sure I will ever have the opportunity to teach, but found that techniques that are common in teaching in two-year college have some similarities with techniques training people in positions at jobs.
  8. Miniportfolio of English in the two-year college assignments. Grade received: 94/100. I enjoyed typing up these sample writing course projects for a teaching English in the two-year college course. These were modeled after some really great assignments I had completed during my own time in undergraduate courses.
  9. Transnational feminism course literature analysis paper. Grade received: 85/100. This is a literature analysis of a fantastic novel for a literature course that I had the opportunity to take as an elective. 
  10. Writing science for the public webtext project. Grade received: 30/30. This was my most intensive effort so far to write science for a lay public. I chose to research and write about fitness trackers; this site contains both long-form and short-form pieces that were sourced from research articles.
